What is this?

Have you ever used the phrase "it's on my radar"? Well this is my radar. It is a living record of the technologies I know about, split into three rings with the following semantics:

  • Expert — Technologies I am most comfortable with, and would use to tackle new problems with high confidence. These are typically technologies I use on a regular, frequent basis.
  • Strong — Technologies I have used with success in multiple projects. These are typically either technologies that I am actively gaining experience in, or technologies that I have used extensively in the past.
  • Competent — Technologies I have invested time into learning, often completing smaller experiments and test projects, but have not fully explored in depth. These could be technologies that I want to learn more about, or have come into contact with whilst working on large systems.

Why?

I primarily use this as a tool to not only keep track of what I know about, but also to help me discover gaps or weaknesses in my knowledge that I can work to improve.

This radar is also a helpful aid that allows me to quickly impart upon other developers or people within the industry the knowledge and skills that I possess. It can concisely answer common questions of "Do you know about X?".

How?

This radar is inspired heavily by similar radars used by OVO Energy, Zalando, and other companies to document technologies used within the company and enable engineers to pick the best new technologies for new projects.

Zalando helpfully open-sourced the code used to generate their radar. This radar is running on a heavily refactored version of their original design, which affords a greater understanding of how the chart works and enables easier modifications, such as adjusting the number of rings.

You can find the source for this radar on GitHub.
